# Limitations

Several limitations should be considered.

## Correlation versus Causation

A statistical association between a change point and a historical event does not prove that the event caused the observed price change.

Multiple economic and political factors may occur simultaneously.

Therefore, this project identifies plausible relationships rather than definitive causal effects.

---

## Model Simplification

The initial Bayesian model assumes a single major change point and changes in the mean price only.

Real financial markets may experience multiple change points and simultaneous changes in volatility.

---

## External Variables

The model does not explicitly include variables such as:

* GDP
* inflation
* exchange rates
* supply and demand
* inventories
* interest rates

These variables may also influence oil prices.

---

## Event Timing

Some geopolitical events influence markets before official announcement dates due to expectations and speculation.

Consequently, detected change points may not exactly match event dates.
